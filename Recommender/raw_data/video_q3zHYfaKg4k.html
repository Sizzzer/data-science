In this video, we continue to part II of the proof of convergence of ADAM. We start by a short review of what we have discussed in part one and dig deeper into the convergence proof. We attempt to detail each and every step while trying to make the exposition clear-enough. This is a math-heavy lecture but detailed step by step in the hope of giving a clear overview of the proof. If you have any questions concerning any step of the presentation, please share them in the comments below and we will be more than happy to have a look and answer them. In short, the contents involve:

0. Thanking all communities who helped us
1. Review of ADAM's Algorithm
2. Proof of L-Smoothness of the objective
3. Handling the ratio of random variables
4. Bounding the terms
5. Choosing proper free parameters
6. Considering two cases for mini-batches
7. Showing that with a constant batch, we can get to an approximate stationary point but not exact even if the number of iterations grew to infinity
8. Showing that with growing batch sizes the above problem is resolved

Slides can be found here: https://docs.google.com/presentation/...

We also have an exercise concerning a proof on the variance of sub-sampled gradients. Please share your answers in the comments below. We will be gladly happy to have a look at these and discuss over them. If you prefer a more discrete discussion, please send your answers to learningmachine38@gmail.com. We will try to get back to you in a matter of 2 days.

Of course, the material is not ours but is taken from the proof in the 2018 NeurIPS paper: https://papers.nips.cc/paper/8186-ada...

We made minor changes to the proof of the NeurIPS paper towards the end when it came to the assumption on the variance. This change is very minor and keeps the original statements made in the NeurIPS paper unchanged. It's just a slightly different route to the proof.

We have varying ideas on what to describe next. Please feel free to fill this anonymous survey for us to know what to detail next: https://www.surveymonkey.co.uk/r/XRRPDBF
We have topics varying from implementations to theories of reinforcement learning, variational inference, and normalising flows.
