Machine learning is often talked about as though it is incomprehensible black magic. Unfortunately, this is currently at least somewhat true. But it's not entirely true!

In this talk, I'm going to try to talk about some of the surprisingly simple ideas and intuitions underlying the general practice of learning from data, how this gives a valuable lens to understand tools like neural networks, and to try to demystify WHY they actually work. And to explore how these ideas can be motivated with simpler and more familiar examples of learning from data, such as linear regression.

I'll be focusing on the ideas and intuitions rather than the mathematical details, so this will be a fairly non-technical talk, and I expect to mostly be of interest to people unfamiliar with machine learning.

I'd love to hear any feedback on the talk, and whether I should do this kind of thing again! https://forms.gle/g4GnCJcV1gsGFiRq6
