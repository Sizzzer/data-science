Brodmann17's Co-Founder & CTO, Dr. Amir Alush talks about improving compute/power efficiency: "The significant potential for improvement is on the software side – or, more specifically – on the deep learning algorithms side."

@ AutoSens Detroit 2019
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every perception solution is a combination of hardware and software. The hardware manufacturers are continuously improving compute/power efficiency, but no big leap forward is expected on that front. The significant potential for improvement is on the software side – or, more specifically – on the deep learning algorithms side.

In this presentation, we will discuss the deep learning stack (cores’ architecture, deep learning framework, inference engine, and algorithm). First, we will review the advantages and disadvantages of the different deep learning frameworks being used today by the machine learning community, with a special focus on efficiency.

Next, we will review the different inference engines and what can be expected from these engines in terms of maturity, flexibility, code size, and memory usage/overheads, as well as the major question of whether or not to write your own inference engine to resolve some of these issues and limitations.
